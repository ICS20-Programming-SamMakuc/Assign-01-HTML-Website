What is an Orangutan?

An orangutan is a great ape that is native to the rainforests of Indonesia and Malaysia, they are one of the great apes, along with gorillas, chimpanzees, bonobos, and humans. They spend most of their time in trees and Orangutans and humans share 97% of their DNA. They are known for their reddish-orange and long, powerful arms, which they use to move through the trees. They are also very intelligent and have been recorded using tools in the wild, such as using sticks to extract insects from tree bark or rocks to crack nuts.
